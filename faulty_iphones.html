Question:

Exactly 3 factories are producing iPhones for sale in the US.
Factory 1 generates 100 phones per hour, with an average of 6 faulty phones per 1000.
Factory 2 generates 400 phones per hour with an average of 1 faulty phone per 1000.
Factory 3 generates 1000 phones per hour, with 2 faults per 1000.
What is the probability that the phone sold in a shop is faulty?
This problems can be solved by using the
Law of Total Probability and Bayes Theorem

Where $P =$ Probability and $F =$ Factory,

and $Bad|F_j =$ is the amount bad per factory,

and $F_j =$ is the amount per factory,

$P(Bad) = {\displaystyle{\sum_{j=1}^3 P(Bad|F_j) P(F_j)}}$

$P(Bad|F_1) = \left( \frac{6}{1000} * 100/hr \right) + $

$P(Bad|F_2) = \left( \frac{1}{1000} * 400/hr \right) + $

$P(Bad|F_3) = \left( \frac{2}{1000} * 1000/hr \right) $

$= \left( (.0006) \frac{100}{1500} \right) + \left( (.0001) \frac{400}{1500} \right) + \left( (.0002) \frac{1000}{1500} \right) $

$= \frac{.6}{1500} + \frac{.4}{1500} + \frac{2}{1500}$

$= \frac{3}{1500} = .002$

$\therefore 0.2\%$ of all iPhones sold are faulty.
